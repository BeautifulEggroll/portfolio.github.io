About Me

Hello! My name is Cameron Cheung. I am an incoming senior at Glen A. Wilson High School. I enjoy teaching others and encouraging little kids to explore the STEM field. I love practicing Kendo and thrive when I feel my skills improving. I sometimes disassemble or modify the electronics in my house to figure out their inner workings. In my spare time, I 3D-model and 3D-print gaming peripherals and accessories. I take pride in the results of my hard work, and love seeing others use and appreciate my designs. I am always searching for ways to improve what I do, from my relationships, teaching others, or 3D modelling.

Reflections

Over the past few years, my journey in robotics has been incredibly rewarding. One of my favorite moments was when our team received the Inspire Award, which felt like a true recognition of our hard work and dedication. Making it through the Illinois Leadership Tournament (ILT) was another highlight that showcased our perseverance and teamwork. Beyond competitions, I grew personally by teaching my two younger siblings how to code in Java through Minecraft. Sharing my passion for programming and seeing them excited to learn was deeply fulfilling.

Looking ahead, I am excited to continue developing my skills in STEM and mentoring others along the way. I want to keep pushing myself both academically and in extracurriculars like robotics and Kendo. I hope to inspire more young people to explore technology and creativity, just like I was inspired. Ultimately, I aspire to pursue a career that combines my love for coding, problem-solving, and helping others grow. This reflection motivates me to keep learning and embracing every opportunity that comes my way.
